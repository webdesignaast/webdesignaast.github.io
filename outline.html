Course Outline

 

Brief

This course is an introduction to Webdesign and Interactive design. It acts as a gateway to the students' first steps into the endless possibilities of todays technology. It is based on the self-learning and de-learning process.The students will be exposed to various movements such as the smart city and the DIY (Do It Yourself) and more.
It is kind of an extended workshop where the student is supposed to design an interactive assistive gadget for the disabled along with designing and creating their own website and learn how to connect their gadget's data to their website. The design process of the gadget design and the website design will begin from day 1 and the student will continue to develop their work in parallel to the tutorials handed by the tutors.

 

The Gadget Project

The students will be required to design a prototype of an interactive assistive gadget for the disabled to assist them in a specific area of their lives.In order to do that you will be asked to experiment this disability yourself. You will begin working on the design from week 1 and will start executing it from week 7 to be submitted by the end of the term.
For a little warm up, here are some movies such as; Music Within / Rory O'Shea Was Here / dancer in the dark and series such as; Black Mirror British TV series.

 

Website Design

The students will be required to design and create their own personal website and linking them to their gadget feeds, and weekly update of their projects including their own work documentation; research, assignments, videos, images, inspirations etc... which will be submitted by the 6th week and then weekly updated to be finally submitted by the end of the term.

 

Requirements

Arduino Uno - starter kit + necessary sensors/actuators for each student's project

 

Ethical contract

This course is based on sharing knowledge. In this course we understand that interactions, communications and discussions are important to facilitate and speed up the learning process, yet we do not expect a student to do someone else's assignment or project as this will be penalized.
